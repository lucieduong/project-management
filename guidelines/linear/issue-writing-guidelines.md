# Issue Writing Guidelines

Simple rules for writing clear, useful issues. Following these helps developers fix issues faster.

---

## How to create issues (including with screenshots)

**Easiest way:** In Cursor, say you want to **create a Linear issue**, then **describe the problem** in your own words. If you have **screenshots** (e.g. from your phone or a screen capture), **paste or upload them** in the same message. Cursor will:

- Fill in as much as possible (title, steps, expected behavior, acceptance criteria) from your description and what it sees in the screenshots.
- Ask you only for what it still needs (usually **which team** and **priority**).
- Create the issue in Linear and, when possible, **add your screenshots to the issue** so they appear in Linear for the developer.

You don’t need to fill a form. Explain what’s wrong and where (and add a screenshot if you have one); Cursor and the guidelines below do the rest.

---

## Always include

- **Page URL** — The full URL where the issue happens. Without it, developers have to guess where to look.
- **Steps to reproduce** — A numbered list so anyone can follow the same steps and see the problem.

## Be specific

- **Expected behavior** — Say exactly what should happen (e.g. "The button should open the cart sidebar"), not "it should work."
- **Problem** — Describe what is wrong in plain language. Avoid vague phrases like "broken," "doesn't work," or "it's buggy." Say what you see and what you did.

## When you can

- **Screenshots or links** — Include them whenever possible. They make the issue much easier to understand.

## Keep it consistent

- **Title** — One short, descriptive line (e.g. "Checkout age verification popup blocks order").
- **Priority** — Use only: Low, Medium, or High.
- **Type** — Use only: Bug, UX Improvement, Feature, or Content.

---

Clear issues mean less back-and-forth and faster fixes.
