# Step 3: Paste in Figma Make and link back to Notion

You have the Figma Make prompt from Step 2. Now paste it into Figma Make, generate the design, and save the link (and the prompt) in your Notion project page.

---

## 1. Paste the prompt in Figma Make

1. Open **Figma** and go to **Figma Make** (or the AI design feature you use).  
2. **Paste** the prompt you copied from Cursor (the whole block the AI gave you).  
3. **Upload** any assets it asks for (e.g. logo, reference images) if you have them.  
4. Click **Generate** (or the equivalent) and wait for the design.  
5. Tweak if you need to (e.g. regenerate with small changes).

---

## 2. Copy the Figma design link

When you’re happy with the design:

1. In Figma, get the **shareable link** to the file or the specific frame (e.g. Share → Copy link).  
2. Keep this link; you’ll paste it in Notion in the next step.

---

## 3. Save the link and prompt in Notion

1. Open the same **project page** in the Website Dev Pipeline (the one you filled in Step 1).  
2. **Paste the Figma design link** in:
   - The **“Figma design link”** section on the page (in the Figma Make area), and  
   - The **“Figma design URL”** property on the **card** (edit the card on the board and set that field).  
3. **Paste the prompt** you used into the **“Prompt used”** section on the page (and optionally into the **“Figma prompt”** property on the card).  
4. **Check off** the Design & mockups items on your checklist and move the card to the next stage if you’re ready.

---

## 4. When you create the Linear issue for the developer

Include the **Figma design URL** in the Linear issue so the developer can open the design in one click. You can also add the link to this Notion project page if that’s useful.

---

## You’re done with the design step

Once the link and prompt are saved in Notion, the design step is complete. Move on to the next stage on your pipeline (e.g. Content & media or Coding) and create the Linear issue when it’s time.
