SECTION 01: The Situation
Heading: Louisiana built the model. Now Washington needs to get the regulation right.
Paragraph 1: Louisiana is one of the largest hemp beverage markets in the country — and one of the most regulated. Age restrictions, product registration, third-party testing, licensed retail. The state built a framework that works, and the industry grew responsibly within it.
Paragraph 2: A provision in a 2025 federal funding bill puts that market at risk, with a ban on hemp-derived THC beverages taking effect November 12, 2026. The goal of this Coalition is not to fight Washington — it's to make sure Louisiana's experience and regulatory track record informs what comes next at the federal level.
Paragraph 3: Smart federal policy already exists at the state level. Louisiana is the proof.

SECTION: Pull Quote
Quote: "This is not a fight against Washington. It is an opportunity to show Washington what responsible regulation actually looks like — and Louisiana has already done the work."

SECTION 02: Who We Need
Heading: Who we're bringing together
Paragraph 1: The Louisiana Hemp Beverage Coalition brings together senior business leaders from hemp, alcohol, grocery, and adjacent industries — people with the expertise, credibility, and relationships to engage policymakers directly and make Louisiana's case at the state and federal level.
Paragraph 2: This is industry-led advocacy, grounded in real business experience and a proven regulatory track record.
Sector Grid:
