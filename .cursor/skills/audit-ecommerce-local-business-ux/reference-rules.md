# Reference: UX Rules for Audit (Local Business & Ecommerce)

**Source:** Baymard Institute (2024–2026). Use only these rules when auditing; do not suggest changes that contradict them.

---

## 1. Local Business

### Trust & Findability
- Footer: **direct links** to "Shipping Info" (or "Delivery") and "Return Policy"; not only under Help/FAQ.
- Use conventional link text ("Return Policy", "Shipping") for scan/find.
- Separate "Return an Item" (action) from "Return Policy" (terms); cross-link or merge.

### Physical Presence & Omnichannel
- **In-store return** same prominence as mail return when physical location exists.
- **Store pickup** in same interface as shipping (e.g. at checkout), not only on product/cart.
- Consider distance / "nearest store" for return or pickup when location known.

### Contact & Reassurance
- If phone required: **explain why** (e.g. "For delivery questions only") and/or make optional. Unexplained required phone increases fake data.
- For lesser-known brands: **trust cues** near sensitive inputs; encapsulate payment; fix layout bugs on key pages.

### Homepage & Scope
- Homepage shows **breadth** of offerings (~40–50% of main product/service types).
- Mobile featured links: **full scope in link text** (e.g. "Women's New Arrivals" not "New Arrivals").

---

## 2. Ecommerce — Homepage & Category Nav

### Homepage
- Featured scope links on mobile: **full scope** in link (e.g. "Women's New Arrivals").
- **Wide selection** of product types (~40%+); not carousel-only for breadth.
- No aggressive/distracting ads above the fold; no pop-ups as main way to convey key info.
- Carousels: 5–7 s/slide (text-light), up to ~10 s (text-heavy); **pause on hover**; **no autorotation on mobile**. Prefer static + featured categories.
- **Clear hit areas**: one link vs several; hover/styling on desktop; single hit area per element on mobile.

### Category Structure
- Categories in **chunks of ~10** per level; deepest level has ≥ ~10 products.
- **Highlight current scope** in main nav (active category distinct).
- **Parent category headers in dropdowns clickable** (e.g. "Shoes" → category page).
- Hover dropdowns: **300–500 ms delay** before open.
- Intermediary category pages: **subcategories as primary content** at top; clear thumbnails; link inspirational imagery to exact products/curated list.

---

## 3. Ecommerce — Product Lists & Filtering

### List Design
- **Combine** color/size/style variations in **one list item** (swatches); not one row per variation.
- **3+ thumbnails** per product in list (2 min); apparel consider 5–15; allow swipe/carousel in list.
- For multi-volume products: show **price per unit** and total.

### Filtering
- Essential when relevant: **Price**, **User ratings**, **Color**, **Size**, **Brand**.
- **Multi-select within same filter** (e.g. Blue + Black); not mutually exclusive only.
- Horizontal filter bars only when **≤ ~8** filters; prefer **sidebar** when many.
- **Applied filters visible** (above list) with "Clear all" and per-filter remove; on mobile: horizontal scroll or stacked at top.

### Sorting
- At least: **Price**, **User rating**, **Best selling**, **Newest**.

---

## 4. Ecommerce — Product Page

### Imagery & Media
- **Thumbnails** for image gallery (not only dots) when multiple images.
- **Navigate reviewer images** (e.g. carousel) without closing each review.
- **Curated social images** (e.g. Instagram) with shoppable links; avoid overload of paid posts.

### Reviews
- Review form: **only highly relevant** questions; avoid long/off-topic surveys.
- **Respond to negative reviews**; style staff replies differently from user content.
- Support **navigation across all reviewer images** (e.g. one carousel).

### Shipping & Cost
- **Estimated shipping cost** or "Free shipping" (with conditions) **on product page**; not only in banners/header.
- "Free shipping" **near Buy/Add to Cart**; state **conditions** clearly; **dynamically update** (e.g. "Spend $X more for free shipping").
- Buy section **focused**; avoid bloating with upsells/warranties/secondary CTAs.

### Out-of-Stock & Discontinued
- **Temporarily out of stock**: allow **order** with **extended delivery date**; short explanation; promote **alternatives**. Don’t rely only on "Email when in stock" or save-to-list (especially if account required).
- **Discontinued**: clearly mark; **prominently promote alternatives** (e.g. replacement) at top or in place of Buy.

## 5. Ecommerce — Checkout

### Account & Guest
- **Guest checkout** offered and **most prominent** on account step. Delay account creation until **after** order; inform users they can create at end.
- **Minimal** password rules (e.g. 6–8 chars); consider passwordless where appropriate.

### Form Fields & Layout
- **Minimize fields**; perceived effort matters. Aim for as few as needed (~12 elements in ideal flows).
- **Single "Full name"** field instead of First/Last.
- **Hide "Address Line 2"** behind link (e.g. "Add Apt, Suite, Floor"); consider address lookup.
- **Hide coupon/promo** behind link; prefer proactively applying discounts.
- Billing: default **"Same as shipping"**; hide billing fields until user opts for different.
- **Mark both** required and optional (e.g. asterisk + "(optional)"); 32% missed required when only optional marked.
- Hide uncommon optional fields behind link; use checkbox for "Same as shipping" etc.
- If phone required: **explain why** inline; else optional or omit.

### Delivery & Fulfillment
- Show **delivery date** (e.g. "Delivery April 4–8"), not only "2–3 business days."
- **Cutoff as countdown** (e.g. "Order in next 43 min to get by Tuesday").
- **All fulfillment options** (ship, store pickup, local delivery) in **one step** so users can switch without going back to cart.

### Cart
- Quantity: **buttons** or **buttons + text field**; avoid plain text field only (wrong quantities, especially on mobile).

### Validation & Errors
- **Adaptive error messages** (exact issue + how to fix); not single generic "Invalid."
- **Preserve entered data** after validation errors.

### Trust & Security (Perceived)
- **Visually encapsulate** payment/card section (border, background, padding); 1–2 trust elements **inside** (padlock, seal).
- **Fix layout bugs** on checkout; they erode trust.

## 6. Ecommerce — Accounts & Self-Service

### Account Menu & Dashboard
- Account menu: direct links to **Dashboard**, **Orders**, **Payment methods**, **Loyalty**, **Wishlist**, **Addresses**, **Password** (not truncated to 2–3).
- Dashboard: paths to **all** account features.

### Stored Payment
- **"Edit" flow** for stored cards (show masked number, one flow to replace); not "Delete then re-add" only.

### Orders & Tracking
- **Tracking on-site** (date, courier, events); don’t send only to carrier site.
- After cancel request: **"Cancellation requested" / "Pending cancellation"** in order history + **confirmation email**.

### Returns
- **In-store return** same prominence as mail in return flow and help/FAQ.
- Footer: direct links to **Return Policy** and **Shipping Info**.

## 7. Mobile-Specific

### Navigation & Taxonomy
- **Highlight current scope** when menu is open.
- **"View all [category]"** at **each level** of category tree.
- When types share most attributes: use **filters** instead of separate top-level categories.

### Search
- Autocomplete: support **abbreviations/symbols** (e.g. "3pc" → "3-piece") and map to same results.
- **Category scope suggestions** in autocomplete (e.g. "[query] in Furniture") when query matches multiple categories.

### Touch & Input
- **Correct input type** (e.g. `inputmode="decimal"` for card, email, tel) for right keyboard.
- **Disable autocorrect** on name, street, city, email where it hurts.
- **Touch target size** matches visible element; links/buttons easy to tap.

### Thumbnails & Product Page
- **3+ thumbnails** per product in lists; swipe/carousel. Apparel: consider 5–15.
- Consider **social-sourced images** on product page.

---

## 8. Accessibility (EAA / WCAG-Aligned)

- **Text alternatives** for images; avoid images of text for critical content.
- **Full keyboard** operability (focus order, visible focus, skip links); nav, filters, list items accessible.
- **Contrast** (e.g. 4.5:1 normal text, 3:1 UI); support user-adjustable text spacing.
- **Forms**: programmatic labels, grouped fields; **errors** perceivable and understandable (not only color); announced to screen readers.
- **Semantic headings** (e.g. `<h1>`–`<h6>` in order); **ARIA landmarks**; clear **page titles**.

---

## 9. Agent Notes (Baymard 2026)

- Generic LLM UX suggestions have **low documented accuracy** (e.g. 50–75%) and can **harm conversion**. Prefer **rule-based, research-grounded** checks.
- Many high-impact fixes are **small** (delivery date, "Place Order" placement, required/optional labeling). Treat small UI details as conversion-critical.
- **Tie suggestions to specific rules**; avoid vague "improve form" or recommendations that contradict the rules above.
